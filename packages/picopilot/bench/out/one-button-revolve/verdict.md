# Jam Verdict: REVOLVE

Theme: **one button** | Time budget: 50 min

## Rubric (1-5)

1. **PLAYABLE - 5.** Boots clean (`boot.json` exitReason `sentinel`, no error), `playable.txt` = ok, `verify` green; the play screenshots show the player dot (blue with white nose + fading trail) clearly visible on the orbit ring in the play state, HUD live (SCORE 0/10, 3 lives), a green pickup telegraphing, and the code has a real goal (10 pts win) and lose (3 lives) with input flipping direction.
2. **THEME FIT - 5.** A pointed, self-aware "one button" interpretation: instead of the obvious tap-to-jump impulse, the single button REVERSES an ongoing orbit, so the one constraint IS the whole mechanic rather than a limit worked around.
3. **MECHANIC ORIGINALITY - 4.** Reverse-your-orbit-direction is a genuinely fresh spin on the crowded one-button space, with real decision layers (green vs gold-bonus risk/reward, combo-restores-life), though "orbit and dodge/collect" isn't wholly unprecedented.
4. **EXECUTION - 5.** Coherent, tightly scoped, 1384 tokens; notably it ships engineered fairness (no-dead-state spawn spacing, speed-scaled reaction window, difficulty ramp) that the JAM.md backs with actual auto-player proofs rather than assumptions, remarkable for the time budget.
5. **POLISH - 4.** Starfield, particle bursts, screen shake, white-flash on reverse, motion trail + nose dot, title and win/lose panels, and structurally-present SFX/music (unauditioned, headless); art is minimal-but-purposeful primitives.

## Overall

This is a real, playable, cleanly themed game. It boots, responds to the one button, has a visible player, a clear goal/challenge/win-lose, and reads well on screen. The standout idea is the theme read itself: making the single button REVERSE a continuous orbit rather than apply an impulse, turning "one button" from a limitation into the entire skill expression (choosing the moment to flip). Execution is unusually disciplined for 50 minutes, with fairness guarantees that are tested, not hoped. The biggest weakness is that the core loop, once understood, is a fairly gentle collect/dodge on a single ring, and the sound is unauditioned; originality is strong but not radical, and the challenge ceiling is modest. Still, this is exactly what a jam should reward: a small, finished, original, provably-playable idea over an ambitious broken one.

## Weighted score: **90 / 100**

(playable 30/30, theme 19/20, originality 25/30, execution 15/15, polish 4/5, rounded)

**Yes** - the reverse-the-orbit hook is instantly graspable and satisfying to flip.
